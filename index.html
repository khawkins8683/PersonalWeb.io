Best Math Story

Evariste Galois was born near Paris, France in 1811, and is concidered one of the greatest mathematicians of the 19th century despite the fact that he only lived to be 20 years old. He is accreditted with initially developing "Galois" Theory and Group Theory. The Standard Model(the so to speak "peridoic table of physics"), is an application of Group Theory in physics.

Galois, father was the town mayor and his mother was well educated and loved classical literature. So Galois recieved a top notch education, but (probably in some part due to his mothers love for the arts) he was not introduced to mathematicis until age 14. So, one of the greatest mathematicians of the 19th centeury died at age 20 and never studied math before age 14.

Once introduced to the subject, Galois imediatedly become a protoje. Reportedly he found a copu of "The Elemenents of Geometry" by Legendre which aparently "read like a novel". More inpressive than reading the novel was the fact that he absorbed the material and which lead to him solving a 350 year old problem, the conditions for soving a polynomial.

Unfortunately for Galois, his mathematical education did not go well. He was rejected from the best school, Ecole Polytechnique, but not before hitting the interviewer in the head with an erasor for being "stupid". Even worse, the French Reveolution was about to break out.

Galois quickly got rapped up in the revolution, expeled form Ecole Normale, and was arrested multiple times. This is where aparently he met and fell fond for the jailors daughtor. One thing lead to another and another and durring his last imprisonment Galois agreed to duel someone (for the girl) upon his release. This is when romatics depict Galois frantically scribling the foundations upon a prison wall. He probably had most of his work done by this time, but it is fair to say he probably knew he was going to die.

After being released from jail Galois faced off in a duel. Predictably, the mathematician lost. This is how a teenager, who started studying mathematics over a course of 6 distracted years managed to change the world forever.
